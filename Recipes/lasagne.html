World's Best Lasagne

The Secret Behind This Classic Lasagna Recipe

From what I can tell, the secret behind Alton's lasagna is the addition of a little sugar to the sauce and using a bit more cheese than most other recipes.

There are a few minor tweaks to his original recipe that I found necessary, for example adding a dash of wine vinegar to the sauce and changing the amount of Parmesan cheese.

Ingredients

  • 2 tsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1/2 medium onion, diced (about 3/4 cup)
  • 1/2 bell pepper, diced (about 3/4 cup)
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1 (28 oz) can tomato sauce
  • 3 oz tomato paste
  • 1 can crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp fresh oregano
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley
  • 1 tbsp Italian seasoning
  • 1 pinch garlic powder
  • 1 tbsp red or white wine vinegar
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • Salt to taste
  • 9 lasagna noodles
  • 15 oz ricotta cheese
  • 1 1/2 lbs mozzarella cheese
  • 1/4 lb freshly grated parmesan cheese

Steps

  1. Put a large pot of seasoned water on the stovetop on high heat.
  2. In a large skillet heat 2 tbsp olive oil on medium heat. Add the ground beef and cook until it is lightly brown on all sides. Remove the beef with a slotted spoon to a bowl. Drain off all but a tbsp of fat.
  3. Add the bell pepper and onions to the skillet Cook for 4 to 5 minutes until onions are translucent. Add the garlic and cook for 30 seconds. Return the ground beef to the pan. Stir to combine, reduce heat to low and Cook for another 5 mins.
  4. Transfer the beef mixture to a medium sized pot Add the chrushed tomatoes, tomato sauce, and tomato paste to the pot. Add parsley, oregano Italian seasoning, garlic powder and red or white wine vinegar. Stir in sugar a tbsp at a time, tasting after each addition. Add salt to taste Bring the sauce to a simmer and then lower the heat to maintain a low simmer. Cook for 15 to 45 mins, stirring often. Remove from heat.
  5. By now the water should be boiling. Add the noodles and cook them to al dente. Stir often to prevent from sticking. Drain and rinse with cold water gently separating any noodles that may be sticking together. Spread a little olive oil on a cooking sheet and coat the noodles with the oil.
  6. Heat the oven to 375 F
  7. In a 9x13-inch casserole dish ladle a cup of sauce over the bottom of the dish. Arrange one layer of noodles lengthwise over the sauce. Ladle a third of the remaining sauce over the noodles. Sprinkle a third to the mozzarella on top of the sauce. Add half the ricotta cheese and then half of the parmesan. Repeat another layer. Finish with another layer of noodles. Spread the reamining sauce over the noodles and sprinkle with the remaining mozzarella.
  8. Cover the pan with foil, tented slightly so it doesn't touch the lasagne. Bake at 375F for 45 mins. Uncover for the last 10 mins to get a crusty top.
  9. Allow lasagne to cool at least 15 mins before serving. Leftovers will keep for about 5 days.
